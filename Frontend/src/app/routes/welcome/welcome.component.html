What is this Coronavirus?

The coronavirus (CoV) is actually a big family of breathing virus which can cause low to medium level sicknesses, from the common cold to a more important syndrome like the MERS (Middle-East Respiratory Syndrome) or the SARS (Severe Acute Respiratory Sindrome) They're called coronavirus because of the crown-shaped spikes found on the molecule surface (corona = crown). The coronaviruses are common in many animal species (such as camels and bats). There is, though, a very small chance that Coronaviruses can evolve and infect a human, and then spread to the population. This new Coronavirus is actually a new branch of the Coronavirus family which has never been identified on a human until this recent pandemic started.

What symptoms can be found on an infected human?

The most common symptoms from a Coronavirus infection on a human include fever, coughs and breathing difficulties. On more severe cases, the infection can cause pneumonia, SARS, kidney failure or even death. In particular, the Coronavirus branch which infects humans cause low to moderate sicknesses located in the upper respiratory tract, like the common cold, which can persist no more than two weeks. Specific symptoms can include: runnying nose, headache, coughs, sore throat and more generally feeling low on energy. Such coronaviruses can sometimes cause sicknesses located in the lower respiratory tract, like bronchitis or pneumonia. Pneumonia in particular is common on people affected by cardio-vascular chronic diseases and/or respiratory chronic diseases, or subjects with weak immune system, like babies and old people.

How can it be transmitted?

The Coronavirus branch which infects humans can be transmitted from two people through saliva contact, coughs, sneezes and direct contact. The most common example is by touching infected hands which have not been washed.The same goes for mouth, nose and eyes, and on some very rare cases faecal contamination. To avoid being infected it is important to keep the protective mask on, keep the 3 ft. security distance, avoid to make crowds and wash your hands often.
